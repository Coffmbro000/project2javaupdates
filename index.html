The Eagles

Who are "The Eagles"?

The Eagles is an American Rock band that were very popular in the 70's just like Pink Floyd. They are very well known as they helped establish country rock genre into the mainstream

The History of the Eagles

The Band formed in the year of 1971 in the city of Los Angeles.

Founded by Glenn Frey (Guitarist/Vocalist), Don Henley (Drummer/Vocalist), Bernie Leadon (Guitars/Vocalist), and Randy Meisner(Bass Guitar/Vocalist). They debuted with their album called "Eagles" (1972) with two hit singles that both were in the top 20 hits in the United States and Canada. Those hit singles being "Take it Easy" and "Witchy Women". In the year of 1974 a new guitarist joined the band that being Don Felder. As years went by they continued to release many hit singles and albums, such as the single "One of These Nights" and another album being "Hotel California" probably one of the most iconic albums of all time.



Discography

The Eagles released quite a few albums throughout the years of their career, Like mentioned before they debuted with the album "Eagles" in (1972). Only one year later they would put out their second studio album "Desperado", although it wasn't as popular as their previous album and the ones to come it still managed to get in the top 40 albums. In 1975 the band would release their third album "One of These Nights" and then only a year after that they would debut one of best selling albums of all time "Hotel California" in 1976 being considered one of the best albums of all Time.
