Van Halen

Who is "Van Halen"?

Van Halen was one of the greatest bands of the 80's hair metal era, Their music is so influential and is spread across all over pop-culture. They are well known for the unique and fascinating instrumental styles, and most importantly the way they performed live and the energy they brought to shows.


The History of Van Halen

Van Halen is an American Rock band that formed in Pasadena, California in 1973. Well known for brinign hard rock into the scene. The band was so impactful they were inducted into the Rock n Roll Hall of Fame in 2007. From 74' to 85' the band was held together by Eddie Van Halen (guitarist), Alex Van Halen (Drummer), Michael Anthony (bassist/vocalist), and fianlly the front man of the band David Lee Roth (lead vocalist).

Their debut album released in 1978 called "Van Halen". They would release more albums as the years went on. In 1985, David Lee Roth left the band leaving the band without a lead singer as he went on to pursue a solo career in the music scene. The band eventually found a replacement for Roth that being Sammy Hagar, with Hagar now in the band they went on to release four platinum albums in the course of eleven years. In 1996 Hagar eventually parted ways with the band leaving them once again without a frontman for the band. Hagar was replaced with Gary Cherone who had a short run with the band as he eventually left a few years later in 1999. The band took a bit of a break for a couple of years until Hagar rejoined the band for tour in 03'. Sammy once again left the band two years later in 05' as Roth came back onto the scene rejoining the band after many years, however once Roth rejoined Michael Anthony left the band and was replaced by Wolfgang Van Halen (Eddie's son) on the bass. The band continued for years until the tragic news spread across the world as Eddie Van Halen passed away in October 2020.


Discography

Van Halens debut album "Van Halen" released in 1978 containing some of their greatest hits including "Runnin' with the Devil" and "Jamies Cryin'". Only a year later would the band release their second studio album "Van Halen II" in March of 1979. Their next big album released in 1984 only few days after New Years day. This is widely believed that this is their best piece of work as in this album they blended in pianos with the songs like "Jump" and "I'll Wait" something that they haven't expiermemted with before.
